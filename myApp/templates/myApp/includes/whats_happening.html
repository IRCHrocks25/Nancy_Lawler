What’s actually happening

Most companies didn’t “adopt AI.” They layered it onto existing work.

AI rarely enters organizations through a single decision. It appears in practical use cases first, then expands across teams before leadership has a shared way to evaluate impact, risk, or quality.

Small decisions scale faster than oversight.

Without shared visibility and ownership, AI increases speed while consistency, security, and accountability quietly erode.

Where it starts

Common entry points

Marketing

Increased content velocity, faster campaign iteration, broader surface area.

Sales & Support

Summaries, suggested responses, and pressure to close faster.

Operations

Scheduling shortcuts, coordination, and process compression.

IT & Legal

Policy creation, access control, and risk containment.

The issue isn’t AI adoption. It’s adoption without a shared operating frame.

Why this is hard

AI doesn't behave
like past technology.

Traditional controls weren't designed for this pace — or this ambiguity.

01

Small actions scale quickly.

A single prompt template, shared across a team, can define how thousands of decisions get framed.

02

Data doesn't move in straight lines.

Inputs from one system become outputs in another. Provenance gets lost before anyone notices it's missing.

03

Outcomes aren't visible when decisions are made.

The gap between action and consequence is wide enough that no one connects cause to effect.

04

Existing controls weren't built for this.

Governance frameworks designed for software procurement don't map onto tools that generate novel outputs at every use.

Blocking tools doesn't work. · Ignoring the problem doesn't work. · Buying more technology doesn't solve it.

What's needed is clarity. Before acceleration.

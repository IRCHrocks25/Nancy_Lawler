Why this is hard

AI doesn’t behave like past technology.

The challenge isn’t adoption. It’s that small decisions scale fast, data flows unpredictably, and traditional controls weren’t built for the pace or ambiguity.

Small actions scale quickly

What starts as one team experimenting becomes a pattern across departments—without anyone intending it.

Data doesn’t move in straight lines

Information travels between tools, vendors, and workflows in ways that aren’t visible at the moment of adoption.

Outcomes aren’t visible when decisions are made

Exposure created today often appears later—downstream, indirect, and hard to trace back to its source.

Traditional controls weren’t designed for this pace

Governance models assume slower adoption and clearer boundaries. AI breaks both assumptions.

Blocking tools doesn’t work. Ignoring the problem doesn’t work. Buying more technology doesn’t solve it.

What’s needed is clarity — before acceleration.
